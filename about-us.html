About us

Saorla Ó Corráin makes her award-winning chocolates in the foodie paradise of Dingle in County Kerry. It is a one woman artisan business, named after the local pre-Celtic goddess, devoted to making fine and unusual chocolates. 

The business was founded in November 2011 but Saorla was dabbling in her kitchen for months before that: perfecting recipes, experimenting with weird combinations and struggling to contain all her ideas into a single business plan! Finally, the chocolates were revealed to the public of Dingle Farmers' Market and tastings were sought and feedback given.

Saorla named the business Dovinia Chocolates. 
Dovinia is the local pre Celtic dark goddess. Maybe she was the local goddess of fertility and protection, as some ogham stones indicate, but nobody really knows. Her name is written on ogham stones throughout Munster and she is mentioned in the Book of Lecan. Corca Dhuibhne, the Irish name for the Dingle Penninsula, means the tribe of Dovinia.

Chocolate has always been associated with divinity. Early legends of many Mesoamerican civilisations have a trickster deity (usually represented as a feathered serpent) stealing the plant from heaven as a gift for the people of earth. The botanical name of the cocoa plant is "theobroma cacao" which literally means food of the gods.

To weave the strands of history and culture together, Dovinia seemed a perfect name!
